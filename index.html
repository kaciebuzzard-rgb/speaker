The Waiting Rooms of My Life

Selected excerpts from my memoir-in-progress—about caregiving, resilience, and the rooms that change us.

I have learned more about myself in waiting rooms than anywhere else in my life. They are places where identity dissolves and survival quietly reshapes you.
Caregiving is not a moment of crisis. It is a long negotiation between love, endurance, and systems never designed for you to survive them.
Time moves forward. Trauma does not. The hallway changes. The fear remembers.
